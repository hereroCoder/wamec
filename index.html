About Me

From the quiet village of Makunda to the vibrant streets of Miami and now residing in the UK, my journey evolved from studying Biomedical Engineering to discovering a true passion for coding. Doubts about shifting careers lingered until my master's completion, where success stories of self-taught developers ignited my confidence.

Armed with a STEM degree and a coding foundation from coursework (including additional classes in C++ and Java during my bachelor's), I knew I had potential. However, being a jack of all trades led me to jump between languages, extending my learning journey.

Building two web applications during Code First Girls with my teammates marked a turning point. From collaborative meetings and brainstorming sessions to design, delegation, and debugging, completing these projects felt incredibly rewarding, echoing the teamwork experienced in biomedical engineering projects.

Applying concepts like Moscow analysis and integrating research into projects became second nature, reviving the relevance of my BME education and highlighting its unique contribution to my skill set.

Throughout this software development journey, I served as a live-in carer for the elderly with diverse conditions from dementia to end-of-life care. The unique demands of the role have been a constant source of learning, pushing me to develop resilience, patience, compassion, and adaptability.

If you're seeking a candidate who not only comprehends the intricacies of coding but also brings a blend of resilience, empathy, and an unwavering passion for continuous learning, I am eager to explore how I can be an asset to your organization. Let's connect and discuss the possibilities.
